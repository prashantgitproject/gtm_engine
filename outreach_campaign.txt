Here's a concise summary of the outreach campaign design:

---

**Outreach Campaign Design — Summary**

The campaign takes a qualified prospect from the account book and runs them through a personalized, multi-channel drip sequence that adapts in real time based on their activity.

---

**How it works (4 layers)**

1. **Enrollment & segmentation** — Prospects scoring ≥ 70 are auto-enrolled. Score band determines sequence intensity: priority (14 touches over 28 days), standard (8 touches over 35 days), or nurture (4 touches over 42 days). Existing HubSpot contacts skip auto-enrollment and go straight to the AE.

2. **Personalization layer** — Every message is generated by Claude (claude-sonnet-4) using the prospect's specific account book row — their signals, evidence, HRIS stack, company size, and all prior touches. No templates, no merge fields.

3. **Signal monitor** — A background process watches each enrolled prospect for LinkedIn posts, job changes, funding events, email opens, and website visits. Hot signals (prospect posts about pain) trigger an immediate action card. Warm signals (email opened 3×, hiring surge) fork the next touch. All signals feed into future message generation as context.

4. **CRM sync** — Every touch, open, click, and signal is written to HubSpot in real time. A positive response auto-creates a deal with a full dossier: all touches sent, all signals fired, and an AI-generated first call angle for the AE.

---

**Channels & sequencing**

Six channels are used across the sequence — not in parallel, but staggered so the prospect experiences a coherent conversation across surfaces. Email is the primary channel (4–5 touches). LinkedIn runs alongside it (connection + 3 DMs). WhatsApp is warm-only, triggered only after a prior positive signal. Reddit and Instagram are community and engagement plays, not cold outreach.

---

**What makes it different from a standard drip**

Every message references real, specific details from that prospect's row. The sequence pauses or forks when the prospect does something — posts, clicks, changes jobs, raises funding. And every touch across every channel shares the same context window, so nothing is repeated and nothing feels disconnected.

---

**Cost**

The lean stack runs around $384–414/month and handles up to 150 prospects. At a 3% meeting rate, cost per meeting booked comes out to roughly $85 — compared to $150–400 via a traditional outbound SDR.

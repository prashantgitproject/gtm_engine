What the account book is
The account book is the output of Stage 3 in the GTM pipeline — a structured, enriched, and scored list of prospects that the outreach engine consumes directly. Each row must contain enough context to generate a hyperpersonalized message without any additional manual research. Think of it as a dossier per prospect, not a contact list.

The four pipeline stages
The account book is built through four sequential passes:
The first is signal matching — you run your defined signal rules (negative reviews, hiring surges, compliance flags, funding events, tech stack changes) across your source data to identify companies that are actively experiencing pain with their current HRIS. This narrows the universe from thousands of ICP-match companies to a manageable list of companies showing real buying signals right now.
The second is company enrichment — once a company is flagged, you enrich it with firmographics (revenue, size, industry, location), current tech stack (what HRIS they're actually using, via BuiltWith), and any additional context like recent funding or headcount changes. Bitscale is the recommended primary tool here, with Apollo as a fallback.
The third is contact discovery — you identify the right person at that company to approach. This is title and seniority-based: CFO or Controller for ADP/Paychex pain at a 200-FTE firm; Owner-operator at a sub-150 firm; VP People or HR Director if the signal is compliance-driven. Apollo handles bulk discovery, Lusha and LinkedIn Sales Navigator handle spot-checks and precision targeting.
The fourth is contact enrichment — you verify that the person is actually reachable. This means finding and verifying their email (waterfall: Prospeo → Hunter → NeverBounce), confirming their LinkedIn profile, and pulling any public statements they've made that are relevant to the signal.

The enrichment waterfall
No single enrichment provider covers everything. The recommended waterfall is: Bitscale as the primary company enrichment layer, Apollo as the gap-fill for contacts, PDL (People Data Labs) as an API fallback for custom fields, Prospeo for LinkedIn-first email finding, Hunter for domain-based email search, and NeverBounce for bulk verification. This waterfall gets you to roughly 95% coverage vs the 80–85% you'd get from a single provider.

The scoring model
Every prospect gets a score from 0 to 100. It has two components. ICP Fit (weighted at 40%) covers industry match, company size, estimated revenue, and geography. Signal Strength (weighted at 60%) reflects which signals were triggered and how strong they are — HRIS dissatisfaction signals score highest (weight 95), followed by hiring surges (82), compliance flags (78), funding events (71), and 1099 volume spikes (65).
Signal carries more weight than fit because a highly-fit account with no signal is not actionable today. Timing matters more than profile match.
Score bands determine outreach urgency: 85–100 gets outreach within 24 hours with full human review; 70–84 gets a standard automated sequence; 55–69 goes into a lighter nurture; below 55 is monitored and re-scored in 30 days.
There are also multipliers on top of the base score — a decision-maker who publicly expressed pain gets +10 flat, a new CFO or HR Director in role under 90 days gets +7, a detected HRIS switch in the last 6 months gets +8, and accounts already in HubSpot pipeline lose 15 points and get routed to the AE instead.

The reasoning field
Each row has a generated 2–3 sentence narrative that answers three questions: why this company, why now, and why this specific person. It is generated by Claude (claude-sonnet-4-20250514) using the signal data, evidence text, contact context, and score as inputs. The output is not a template — it is specific to that account. A good reasoning field reads like a smart analyst wrote it after doing real research, because in effect, that's what the pipeline is automating.

Build path
For getting started quickly, the recommended stack is Bitscale for company and contact enrichment, Apollo as a gap-fill layer, Prospeo and Hunter for email verification, NeverBounce for bulk verification cleanup, n8n for workflow orchestration, the Claude API for scoring reasoning generation, and HubSpot for CRM write-back. This lean stack runs roughly $800–830 per month and produces around 80–120 qualified prospects per month at a cost of $8–15 per prospect — compared to $25–60 per prospect for a manually researched SDR-sourced list at comparable quality.
